## 1. Introduction for the Layperson

Most people want to help their community, but "paying attention" to local politics takes a lot of time and effort. Most companies want to prove they are "good neighbors," but they don't have a good way to show it.

The **Civic Dividend Protocol** is a bridge between the two. Think of it like a **"Loyalty Program for Democracy,"** where your local representative (or a company) sponsors a community goal, and your "vote" or "feedback" helps unlock funding for that goal.

## 2. How It Works (The 3-Step Dance)

### Step A: The Sponsor Sets a Goal

A corporation (like a local grocery chain or a tech company) wants to prove they care about the neighborhood. They set aside a "Dividend Pool" (e.g., $10,000). They choose a civic issue, like "Redesigning the Main Street Park."

### Step B: The Constituent Signs In (Verified)

A constituent opens PoliTickIt. Because our system has already verified their residence (via the **Sovereignty Ledger**), the app knows they actually live in the affected neighborhood. They see a "Sponsored Pulse" about the park.

- They take a 30-second action: Choosing their favorite park design.
- Or they sign a **Consensus Ripple** supporting a new bike lane.

### Step C: The Dividend is Released

Instead of the constituent getting $5 in their pocket (which could look like "buying votes"), their action earns **Participation Credits**. When the community hits a milestone (e.g., 2,000 verified responses), the Sponsor releases the $10,000 **Civic Dividend** to a local cause, like the community center or a school's library.

## 5. Participation Credits vs. Dividends

- **Participation Credits**: These are "digital badges" the user keeps in their app. They represent their "Civic Weight." The more credits you have, the more "Signal Intensity" your voice has in the representative's dashboard.
- **Civic Dividends**: This is the actual money or resources provided by the Sponsor to the community.
