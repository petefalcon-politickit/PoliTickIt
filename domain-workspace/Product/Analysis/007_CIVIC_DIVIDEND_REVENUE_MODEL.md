## 1. Introduction: The Economics of Participation

The **Civic Dividend Protocol** (CDP) transforms civic engagement from a "cost center" (time spent by citizens, marketing spent by corporations) into a **Value-Generating Asset**. This document outlines how PoliTickIt monetizes this protocol while maintaining the "Truth Continuum" and radical neutrality.

## 2. Primary Revenue Streams

### A. The "Convenience & Verification" Fee (Transactional)

When a Sponsor (Corporation/Foundation) creates a "Dividend Pool," PoliTickIt acts as the verification escrow.

- **Model**: 5% - 8% of the total Dividend Pool.
- **Logic**: Covers the cost of **Hardware Attestation**, ZK-Signature processing, and the generation of a **Forensic Proof Report**.
- **Example**: A $10,000 "Park Improvement" sponsor pays a $500 - $800 service fee.
- **Why it scales**: As "Social Impact" budgets move from generic charity to verified CDP engagement, this becomes a high-volume transaction engine.

### B. ESG Compliance Reporting (SaaS Subscription)

Institutional sponsors require detailed, audit-ready data to satisfy ESG (Environmental, Social, Governance) requirements.

- **Model**: $5,000 - $25,000 / annually per institution.
- **Features**:
  - **Auditor Dashboard**: Direct access for third-party auditors (PwC, Deloitte, etc.) to verify the participation ledger.
  - **Impact Heatmaps**: Visualization of where engagement occurred relative to business operations.
  - **Deterministic Sentiment Analysis**: Moving beyond "mentions" to verified resident consensus.
- **Market**: Fortune 500 CSR (Corporate Social Responsibility) departments and ESG funds.

### C. Participation Credit Micropayments (Micro-transactions)

For high-frequency, low-barrier engagement (e.g., weekly "District Pulses"), sponsors can pay per **Participation Credit** generated.

- **Model**: $0.25 - $1.00 per verified resident response.
- **Logic**: This provides a lower barrier to entry for local small businesses to sponsor smaller, hyper-local dividends (e.g., "Free coffee for the first 100 library volunteers").
- **Revenue**: High-frequency volume across thousands of local businesses.

### D. The "Marketplace" Listing Fee

Non-profits and community causes compete for Dividend Pool allocations.

- **Model**: Freemium for small groups; $99/mo for "Priority Listing" and advanced campaign tools.
- **Features**: Ability to "Push" their cause to active residents who have high Participation Credit scores.
- **Logic**: Encourages non-profits to bring their own members into the PoliTickIt ecosystem, acting as a growth engine for our user base.

## 3. The Revenue Virtuous Cycle

1. **User Growth**: Residents join to unlock dividends (park toys, school books).
2. **Data Density**: More users = higher fidelity "Consensus Ripples."
3. **Institutional Demand**: Lobbying firms and corporations pay more for the high-density, verified data now concentrated on the platform.
4. **Dividend Expansion**: More revenue allows for larger pools, attracting more users.

## 5. Risk Mitigation & Neutrality

To avoid "Buying Influence" concerns (the perception of corporate capture):

1. **Separation of Concerns**: Sponsors _fund_ the pool, but the _community_ (verified residents) determines the outcome via the Consensus Ripple.
2. **ZK-Sovereignty**: Sponsors never get the names/addresses of participants—only the "Fact of Residency" and the aggregate sentiment data.
3. **Threshold Gates**: Dividends are only released upon hitting a **Verified Participation Threshold** (e.g., 500 local residents), ensuring a single loud group can't "game" the system.
