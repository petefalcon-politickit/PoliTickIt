### 2. Theoretical Framework: The Refinement Heuristic ($RS$)

At the core of the **Context Enrichment Processor (CEP)** lies the $RS$ formula, designed to quantify the "Drill-Down" necessity of a data point:

$$RS = (I \times 0.4) + (G \times 0.3) + (R \times 0.3)$$

Where:

- **$I$ (Intensity)**: Magnitude of the event (e.g., policy change vs. procedural update).
- **$G$ (Geographic Density)**: The spatial concentration of the data (National vs. Zip-bound).
- **$R$ (ROI Potential)**: The direct fiscal or resource impact on the constituent.

The architecture enforces a ternary distribution model:

1. **$RS < 0.4$ (Knowledge Tier)**: Retained at the National level for broad awareness.
2. **$0.4 \le RS < 0.7$ (Insight Tier)**: Refined to the State level for regional correlation.
3. **$RS \ge 0.7$ (Action Tier)**: Threaded down to the Congressional District for forensic auditing.
